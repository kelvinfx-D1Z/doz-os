What Is DOZ OS?

The purpose, philosophy, and architecture of the system.

DOZ OS (Digit One Zero Operating System) is the backend of your company. It is not just software — it is the operating system that helps Digit One Zero grow from a founder-dependent business into a scalable, process-driven organization targeting ₦500M+ annual revenue by 2028.

Every module, dashboard, AI interaction, and report exists to answer one question: "What is the next highest-impact action that moves the company closer to the vision?"

The Fundamental Principle
DOZ OS never simply reports what happened. It actively helps the founder and team make better decisions. Every metric has a current value, target, forecast, and required weekly pace. The system always answers: "Are we ahead or behind?"

The system serves four types of users: the Founder (Kelvin), who sees the company-wide growth dashboard; Staff (operations, finance), who manage projects and approvals; Interns, who receive daily tasks and file reports; and Freelancers, who see crew assignments and deliverables. Each role sees a personalized dashboard — everyone knows what they should be doing.

Clients access a separate Client Portal (no login required) where they view project progress, approve deliverables, and confirm payments. Vendors do not have accounts — the Production Manager adds vendor information directly.

The score is calculated from five sub-metrics:

  • Projects without founder (25 pts) — % of projects delivered without your direct involvement as manager
  • Delegated decisions (20 pts) — % of approvals not made by you
  • Strategy vs operations time (20 pts) — ratio of strategy hours to operations hours
  • SOP-driven tasks (15 pts) — % of tasks completed without escalation to you
  • Revenue without founder sales (20 pts) — % of revenue from non-founder-sourced leads

The card also shows your time allocation for the week: how many hours you spent on Sales, Operations, Administration, Strategy, and Delivery. Use the "Log Time" button to record your time — the score updates automatically.

Procurement & Financial Control

How the system prevents financial leakage.

The procurement workflow enforces 3-way segregation of duties: no single person can request, approve, and pay the same transaction. This is enforced both in the database schema and in the API (returns HTTP 403 on violation).

1
RFQ created — staff or PM creates a Request for Quote for a needed item or service
2
Quotes collected — minimum 2-3 vendor quotes (by threshold: under ₦100K = 1 quote, ₦100K-₦1M = 2, above ₦1M = 3)
3
Quote comparison — system highlights the lowest and recommended quote with budget variance
4
Purchase Order issued — formal PO created for the selected vendor
5
Payment Request — requester creates a payment request linked to the PO
6
Approval — approver (≠ requester) approves or rejects
7
Payment — payer (≠ approver, ≠ requester) executes payment and marks as paid
8
Verification — receipt uploaded, expense verified

The Financial Intelligence module shows profit by project, client, and service line. It tracks invoices (with overdue detection and AI-drafted WhatsApp/Email reminders), expenses (with receipt upload), budgets (with utilization bars), and cash flow. A "Reminders" tab shows overdue invoices with copy-ready reminder messages and pending payment confirmations from the client portal.

The Client Portal

How clients self-serve without logging in.

Clients access their portal via a shareable link containing a token (e.g., /?portal=lcc-portal-2025). No DOZ OS account needed. The portal shows only client-facing data — no internal costs, profit margins, or team information.

The portal has three tabs:

  • Projects — view project status, event date, venue, progress, and deliverables. Clients can approve or request changes on each deliverable (with a note).
  • Invoices — view invoice code, amount, amount paid, balance, and status. If there's an outstanding balance, clients click "Confirm Payment" to submit a payment confirmation (amount, method, reference) which goes to the founder for verification.
  • Payment Confirmations — track the status of submitted confirmations (PENDING → VERIFIED or REJECTED).

To share a portal link: go to CRM & Sales → Accounts tab and click "Copy Link" next to any account with an active portal. Five portal tokens are currently active: GTBank, MTN, Lagos Chamber, Dangote, and Shell.

Field Mode for On-Site Work

Mobile-first tools for interns and freelancers at events.

Field Mode is designed for phone-in-hand use at events. It has two features:

Quick Daily Report Filing
File an end-of-day report in under 30 seconds: what you did (one per line), what's planned tomorrow, blockers, hours worked (slider), and mood (emoji: Great/OK/Stressed). Submitting twice today updates the same report — no duplicates.
Offline Event Run-Sheet
A per-project checklist that works without internet. Combines DB milestones with an 11-item event-day template. Milestone toggles queue when offline and auto-sync when back online. Shows progress ("7 of 12 complete") with online/offline indicator.

Daily & Weekly Rhythm

How to use DOZ OS as your operating rhythm.

Every Morning (5 minutes)

2. Check the Founder Freedom Score — is your admin time high?
3. Review Growth Targets — anything red needs attention.

2. Contact every overdue referrer ("Log Contact").

1. Read the Monthly Board Report (auto-generated).
2. Run the Monthly Close routine.
